Redesign of WWF's Australia website and mobile version

Project Overview

Challenge

This project aims to investigate ways to make wildlife conservation more accessible to the general public. To accomplish this objective, the current website needs to be reconstructed and updated with new features.

Research

Research enables me to dig deep into my understanding of users - not only their immediate frustrations, but also their hopes, fears, abilities, limitations, reasoning, and goals. It lays essential foundations for creating solutions in later stages.

Background

WWF-Australia is a part of the global WWF network, and it works to conserve Australia's unique biodiversity and ecosystems. The organization has a range of initiatives and programs aimed at addressing the major environmental challenges facing Australia, such as habitat loss, climate change, and water scarcity One of the strengths of the WWF website is its clean and intuitive design. The navigation menu is easy to use and provides quick access to key sections of the website, such as news, conservation efforts, and ways to get involved. The website's search function is also effective in helping visitors find specific information.

Research Goals

By conducting this research, WWF can gain insights into how users interact with their website and identify opportunities for improving the user experience and engagement. The findings can help the organization better communicate its message and achieve its wildlife conservatio

Research Objectives:

Identify user needs and pain points on the WWF website Evaluate the effectiveness of the website's design and layout Identify opportunities for improving the user experience and engagement Assess the website's accessibility and usability for different user groups Identify areas for improvement in the website's content and messaging

Research Methods:

Online surveys: To gather quantitative data on user demographics, behavior, and satisfaction with the website Interviews: To gather qualitative data on user needs, preferences, and opinions about the website Competitor analysis: Can help WWF understand the strengths and weaknesses of other non-profit organizations in the environmental protection. Heuristic evaluation: By analyzing the product based on established usability heuristics, we can gain a more objective perspective on its strengths and weaknesses, and make informed decisions about how to improve its overall usability.

Interview

To understand people's opinion about supporting wildlife animals, we have conducted 9 virtual interviews via Zoom conference.

Research Synthesis

To analyze the subjective information obtained from user interviews, we utilized an empathy map as a tool to detect commonalities among users, discover valuable observations, and generate requirements.
